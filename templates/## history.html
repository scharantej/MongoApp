Mongolian History Timeline

  • 1206: Genghis Khan unifies Mongolia and establishes the Mongol Empire.
  • 1211-1227: Mongol Empire expands across Asia, conquering China, Persia, and parts of Eastern Europe.
  • 1229: Genghis Khan dies, and his son Ögedei Khan becomes the new emperor.
  • 1235-1241: Ögedei Khan continues to expand the Mongol Empire, conquering Korea and parts of Russia.
  • 1242: Ögedei Khan dies, and his son Güyük Khan becomes the new emperor.
  • 1246: Güyük Khan dies, and his cousin Möngke Khan becomes the new emperor.
  • 1258: Möngke Khan dies, and his brother Kublai Khan becomes the new emperor.
  • 1271: Kublai Khan establishes the Yuan Dynasty in China.
  • 1294: Kublai Khan dies, and his grandson Temür Khan becomes the new emperor.
  • 1368: The Yuan Dynasty is overthrown by the Ming Dynasty in China.
  • 1691: Mongolia becomes a protectorate of the Qing Dynasty in China.
  • 1911: Mongolia declares independence from China.
  • 1921: Mongolia gains full independence.

Important Historical Figures

  • Genghis Khan: Founder of the Mongol Empire and one of the most successful military leaders in history.
  • Ögedei Khan: Second emperor of the Mongol Empire and son of Genghis Khan.
  • Güyük Khan: Third emperor of the Mongol Empire and son of Ögedei Khan.
  • Möngke Khan: Fourth emperor of the Mongol Empire and son of Tolui, the youngest son of Genghis Khan.
  • Kublai Khan: Fifth and final emperor of the Mongol Empire and founder of the Yuan Dynasty in China.
  • Temür Khan: Grandson of Kublai Khan and second emperor of the Yuan Dynasty.
